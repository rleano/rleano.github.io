Conflict Complexity

Software today is hardly ever written by a single developer. Distributed development is a norm and tools such as Git have come to simplify this new way of building software. However, it also brought new challenges such as solving conflicts between developers and version management.

Tools in workspace awareness allow developers to realize the occurrence of conflicts as soon as they appear to deal with them. But they do not say anything about the complexity of the detected conflict. Ideally, developers may prefer to interrupt their work only when it is strictly necessary. That is, busy developers could ignore simple conflicts and only deal with the ones that are getting out of hand (i.e. difficult conflicts).

Crowd Tasking

The human work force of the internet –The Crowd– has shown great potential both in research and industry. Sites likes Amazon’s Mechanical Turk, ODesk, and Fivver are examples of communities based on recruiters posting jobs and workers quickly giving results.

While obtaining the right crowd for a given task and ensuring quality of results. The crowd can prove to be great resource for software development as well. Open Source Development is a smaller crowd that works similarly and identifying small, source code related tasks can prove beneficial to the community to welcome new developers.
